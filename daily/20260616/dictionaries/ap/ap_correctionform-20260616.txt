comment = —Ghost Word!   The headword kuṭṭihārikā (with the double retroflex 'ṭṭ' and the vowel 'i') is a definitive typographical error in this print edition of Apte's dictionary. Ironically, the editor inadvertently provides the correction right next to it by suggesting the cross-reference cf. kuṭahārikā. LB The authentic, correctly spelled word is kuṭahārikā. There is no grammatical basis for the prefix kuṭṭi- here; the misprint likely occurred due to a compositor confusing it with the nearby entries derived from the root kuṭṭ (to pound/crush). LB Here is the precise morphological breakdown: LB   •  kuṭa (कुट): A water-pot, pitcher, or jug. LB   •  hārikā (हारिका): A female carrier, fetcher, or bearer. This is an agent noun derived from the verbal root hṛ (to carry, fetch, or take away), properly inflected with a feminine suffix. LB The Semantic Shift LB   •  Literal Meaning: "A female carrier of water-pots." LB   •  Applied Definition: "A maid-servant" or "female slave."
