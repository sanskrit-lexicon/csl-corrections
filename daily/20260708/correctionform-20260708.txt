comment = Typo – In the Pāṇinian system, “yaṇ” is a pratyāhāra (phonological abbreviatory term) encompassing the semivowels (y, v, r, l). It is absolutely never a secondary affix. The affix cited by APTE must be either “ṇya” for masculine definitions or “ṣyañ” for neuter definitions. The affix (ṣyañ) carries the sense of "the action or state of" (tasya karma bhāvo vā). Therefore, (kāvyam) is the objective, aesthetic creation. The affix (ṇya) carries the sense of "descendant of" (tasya apatyam). Therefore, (kāvyaḥ) is a living, masculine entity.
