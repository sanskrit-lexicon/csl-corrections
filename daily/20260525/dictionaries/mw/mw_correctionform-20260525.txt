comment = Typo – Please note that this is a suggested "Revision" to the Erroneous Listing made here by Monier Williams. Here is the Conclusive Information regarding this much needed "Revision" : LB The designation "√kṛ (2)" is indeed an erroneous classification  found in some dictionaries (like Monier-Williams), which conflates a distinct verbal behavior into a theoretical root. LB 1. The Error of "√kṛ (2)" LB Standard Pāṇinian grammar does not recognize a Class 3 root √kṛ meaning "to praise." The entry often indexed as √kṛ (2) is a lexicographical fiction created to account for Vedic forms that look like Class 3 reduplications but actually belong to a different system. LB   •  The Mistake: Older dictionaries listed a separate root √kṛ (Class 3, Parasmaipada) to explain forms like cākarti and cakrat. LB   •  The Grammatical Reality: These forms are Intensive (Yaṅ-luk) conjugations of the Class 6 root √kṝ (to scatter). The reduplication is an internal feature of the Intensive mood, not a sign of the Juhotyādi (3rd) Class.
